Your eCrew companion.

Built by a captain, for the crew that lives out of a kitbag. Roster, logbook, fatigue tracker, layover map, and opt-in roster sharing with your colleagues — all on your iPhone, all private.

What's in Flyers

  • Roster — month view with block / duty / off / flight-day totals, date-range filter, jump-to-today.
  • Flight detail — crew listing (with heavy-crew pairing variations), great-circle route map, registration, aircraft type, load, layover hotel.
  • Logbook — auto-built 12-month history with Aerolog-compatible export.
  • Stats & FTL — block, duty, and flight-time-limit totals across rolling windows.
  • Expiry tracking — licences, medicals, recurrent training countdown.
  • Fatigue tracker — daily sleep log with 7-day chart.
  • Layover & airport info — local time, weather, sunrise/sunset, hotel notes.
  • Crew Mail — read-only mirror of your eCrew inbox.
  • Live Activities — next-duty countdown on the Lock Screen and Dynamic Island.

Flyers Pro

Flyers Pro unlocks the full app — your roster, flight detail, stats & FTL, expiry tracking, crew mail, fit-for-duty, pay estimate, fatigue tracker, logbook, and private cross-airline roster sharing — with a 7-day free trial. $2.99/month or $19.99/year. Cancel anytime through your Apple ID.

Roster sharing (Flyers Social) lets you share your schedule with the colleagues who matter to you — pilot, crew, friend, partner — even when they fly for a different airline. Cross-airline, opt-in, end-to-end via Apple's CloudKit. Your data never sits on a third-party server.

New in 3.0

Flyers 3.0 is a complete redesign — a luminous, light "flight-deck at dawn" look with a living-horizon next-duty hero (Dark, Airline, and Night Ops still in Settings) — plus in-app Report Fit for Duty for your next duty without opening the eCrew web portal.

Privacy first

Flyers talks only to your airline's eCrew portal and to your own iCloud. No analytics, no third-party SDKs, no telemetry, no advertising. Your credentials live in the iOS Keychain on your device only.
